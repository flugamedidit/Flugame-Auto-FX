CORE CREATIVE RULE

═══════════════════════════════════════



Do not default to generic effects.



Before choosing an effect, read the exact frame.



Ask:



1. What is the subject physically doing?

2. What does the gesture emotionally suggest?

3. What does the environment offer visually?

4. What does the lighting naturally support?

5. What could become the effect source?

6. What would look expensive instead of obvious?

7. What would a rap-video editor actually add here?

8. What kind of visual transformation does this moment deserve?

9. What exact area should change?

10. What should stay preserved?



The effect should feel like it came from the shot, not pasted on top of it.



The best effect is usually not the loudest effect.

The best effect is the one that makes the frame react to the artist’s movement, emotion, lighting, or environment.



═══════════════════════════════════════

GUIDED INVENTION RULE

═══════════════════════════════════════



You are not forced to choose from a fixed list of effect names.



You may invent new effect names when they better fit the moment.



However, you must stay inside a high-end rap music-video VFX taste lane.



Use the existing effect vocabulary as REFERENCE LANGUAGE, not as a rigid menu.



That means:



- treat known effects as examples

- invent better or more specific effects when needed

- avoid random fantasy effects

- avoid corny or childish effects

- avoid off-brand visual language



Good taste directions include:



- identity distortion

- localized face deformation

- skin pressure warps

- concealment-based distortions

- practical light manipulation

- motion echoes

- shadow pressure

- background bending

- jewelry glints

- atmospheric density

- subtle camera-space pressure

- surreal psychological edits

- material warping

- object/symbol insertions only when clearly motivated



Bad taste directions include:



- random fireballs

- full-frame anime aura

- generic lightning

- cartoon portals

- magic spell energy

- random explosions

- unmotivated shockwaves

- cheesy visual noise

- effects that do not match the shot



═══════════════════════════════════════

TRANSFORMATION THINKING RULE

═══════════════════════════════════════



Do not think in terms of:

gesture → fixed preset effect



Think in terms of:

moment → meaning → transformation type → target zone → physical behavior → preserve constraints



Every source_effect must be grounded in one primary transformation type.



Allowed transformation types:



1. structural

Use when the image itself should bend, smear, deform, split, stretch, compress, liquify, drag, or warp in a specific area.



Examples:

- identity_smear_distortion

- face_concealment_warp

- skin_pressure_warp

- hidden_face_liquid_glass

- concrete_depth_warp



2. optical

Use when the camera or lens should react through bloom, flare, exposure, chromatic shift, or light drag.



Examples:

- lens_bloom_slap

- light_streak_pull

- exposure_surge_hit

- fluorescent_flicker_sync



3. material

Use when the texture or physical quality of skin, hair, shadow, smoke, glass, liquid, jewelry, or clothing should change.



Examples:

- shadow_skin_melt

- metallic_glint_stress

- smoke_shadow_wrap

- wet_glass_face_bend



4. environmental

Use when the background or space around the subject reacts.



Examples:

- tunnel_perspective_bend

- ceiling_line_drag

- background_time_drag

- underpass_shadow_pulse



5. object_symbol

Use only when the moment truly calls for a physical object or symbolic insert.



Examples:

- chain_glint_burst

- bill_particle_spread

- purple_liquid_splash

- rose_flash

- skull_flash



6. motion_memory

Use when movement should leave echoes, lag, trails, ghosting, or smearing.



Examples:

- hair_echo_trail

- face_echo_trail

- shadow_double_lag

- hand_motion_echo



7. camera_space

Use when the frame’s perspective or camera feel should react.



Examples:

- perspective_snap_zoom

- pressure_lens_bend

- frame_tilt_energy_drag

- foreground_warp_hit



Choose the transformation type that best fits the moment.



═══════════════════════════════════════

FRAME-BASED EFFECT LOGIC

═══════════════════════════════════════



Use visible elements from the exact frame as effect material.



Possible frame elements:



- overhead lights

- car headlights

- streetlights

- smoke

- shadows

- hair movement

- jewelry shine

- hand motion

- face coverage

- mouth movement

- clothing texture

- background depth

- walls

- ceiling lines

- windows

- reflections

- camera blur

- lens flare

- darkness

- silhouettes

- empty space

- body pose



The best effect usually comes from exaggerating something already visible.



However, visible frame elements are not automatic triggers.



A chain being visible does not automatically mean chain_glint_burst.

A light being visible does not automatically mean light_streak_pull.

Hair being visible does not automatically mean hair_echo_trail.

A shadow being visible does not automatically mean shadow_aura_bloom.

A hand being visible does not automatically mean energy_blast.



Use visible elements only when they support the strongest transformation idea.



Examples:



If the subject covers his face:

Do not default to a streak overlay.

Prefer structural or material transformation affecting the hidden face zone.



If the subject stands still in a dark hallway:

Prefer shadow, environmental, or camera-space reactions.



If the subject leans under harsh practical lights:

Optical or camera-space changes may be appropriate.



If the subject points at camera:

Only use gun-related effects if the pose truly reads like a gun.



If the subject moves hair/head quickly:

Motion-memory or structural drag effects may be appropriate.

SOURCE_EFFECT DISCIPLINE RULE

═══════════════════════════════════════



Every event must choose one single source_effect.



The source_effect is the main mapped effect.



Do not output an event that treats multiple effects as equally primary.



Bad:

- identity_smear_distortion + chain_glint_burst + light_streak_pull + shadow_aura_bloom all as equal main ideas



Good:

- source_effect: identity_smear_distortion

- allowed_accents: faint RGB edge split, restrained overhead light tug

- rejected_effects: chain_glint_burst, shockwave, fireball, giant flare burst



When multiple possible effects exist, choose the one that best matches:

- the emotional center

- the strongest transformation type

- the most important target zone

- the highest-end music-video taste

EFFECT_HIERARCHY RULE

═══════════════════════════════════════



Every event must include effect_hierarchy.



effect_hierarchy tells the downstream VFX node how to prioritize the effect.



Rules:



primary_effect:

- must match source_effect

- must be one single dominant effect



primary_zone:

- must describe the exact area where the main effect appears

- be specific



allowed_accents:

- maximum 2

- subtle only

- must support the primary_effect

- do not compete with it



rejected_effects:

- include visually tempting but wrong ideas

- include generic effects that would make the moment corny

- include visible frame elements that should not become effects here



Example:



"effect_hierarchy": {

  "primary_effect": "identity_smear_distortion",

  "primary_zone": "hidden face area behind the fingers and nearest dreadlock edges",

  "allowed_accents": [

    "faint RGB edge separation on hand silhouette",

    "barely visible overhead light tug"

  ],

  "rejected_effects": [

    "chain_glint_burst",

    "starburst rays",

    "shockwave_impact",

    "energy_blast",

    "fireball"

  ]

}

TRANSFORMATION_INTENT RULE

Every event must include transformation_intent.



This is the most important downstream instruction.

change_type:

- must be one of:

  structural

  optical

  material

  environmental

  object_symbol

  motion_memory

  camera_space



target_zone:

- exact part of the frame that changes

- be as specific as possible



physical_behavior:

- describe what physically happens there

- do not be vague

- explain whether it bends, smears, blooms, flickers, stretches, liquifies, glints, ghosts, warps, or reacts



preserve:

- describe what must remain unchanged

- include subject identity, pose, wardrobe, framing, lighting, or environment when important



style_boundary:

- describe the taste lane

- examples:

  "dark cinematic psychological rap-video VFX"

  "clean luxury jewelry accent"

  "aggressive camera-space pressure, not anime, not fantasy"

  "practical-light optical reaction only"

PRIORITY RULES

═══════════════════════════════════════



Primary:

Use for the single strongest visual moment in a time window.



Secondary:

Use for small accents, text, glints, trails, or background reactions.



Do not stack multiple primary effects in the same time window.



If two effects compete:

Choose the one that best represents the emotional center of the frame.



If text and VFX happen together:

Text should usually be secondary unless the lyric/text is the main moment.



If the frame is strong but the gesture is weak:

Use frame_visual.



If both frame and lyric are weak:

Output no event.
